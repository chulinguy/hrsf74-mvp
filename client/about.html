About the author/creator of the website ...

I am a (self-described) intermediate tango nuevo dancer who leads and follows. True to Bay Area quirkiness, I very much enjoy switching lead/follow within a tanda/song. I like modern songs and will often play tango steps in my head to pop songs from the radio. Tango concepts I am currently working on (as of summer of 2017):

  1. As a leader: engaging my left shoulder blade more; peaking down at my follower's feet less
  2. As a follower: "taller" back-ochos; back crosses with less height change
  3. As a dancer: finding more ways to be a weird dancer to make my partner smile more

The good part of my "tango philosophy" is largely influenced by the following teachers (in no particular order):

Homer & Cristina Ladas (Bay Area), Glenn Corteza (Bay Area), Mira Barakat (Bay Area), Agnes Tang (Taipei), Felipe Martinez & Ayano Yoneda (Bay Area), Mila Salazar (Bay Area), Neeraj Korde (Bay Area), Andrea Fuchilieri (Bay Area)
